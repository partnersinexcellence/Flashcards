Deep Dive: While external costs of mediocrity include missed opportunities and stalled careers, the internal cost is often more significant. You know when you're not performing to your potential, and this creates a kind of professional depression over time. Work becomes something you endure rather than something that engages you. You lose the satisfaction that comes from genuine accomplishment because fulfillment comes from the effort to excel, not from coasting. This erosion of self-respect is the hidden price of settling for "good enough."
