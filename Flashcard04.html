What is the internal cost of mediocrity that's often more significant than external costs?

Short Answer
The internal cost is the erosion of self-respect that comes from knowing you're not performing to your potential.
Deep Dive
Deep down, you know when you're not performing to your potential. Every time you settle for less than your capability, something inside you diminishes slightly. Over time, this creates a kind of professional depression where work becomes something you endure rather than something that engages you.
